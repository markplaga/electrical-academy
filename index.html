Career roadmap

Certification and licensing

Most electricians enter through an apprenticeship. Licensing requirements and exam eligibility vary by state and locality.

Typical U.S. pathway

1
Build prerequisites
High-school diploma or equivalent, algebra, valid identification, and physical readiness.
2
Enter training
Apply to a registered apprenticeship, union program, contractor program, or technical school.
3
Log supervised hours
Complete classroom instruction and paid on-the-job training under qualified supervision.
4
Meet local eligibility rules
Document experience, education, and any required trainee registration.
5
Pass the required exam
Study the adopted NEC edition plus state and local amendments.
6
Maintain the credential
Renew on time and complete continuing education when required.
